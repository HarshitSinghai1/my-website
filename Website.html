About Me

I am a Dynamic Global Should Cost Engineer with over 7 years of experience in cost estimation, modeling, and project cost engineering within the manufacturing sector. My expertise spans diverse manufacturing processes including fabricated parts, castings, forgings, truck systems, and electricals, with a strong background in CNC Machining, DFM, and automation.

Throughout my career at companies like Oshkosh AeroTech, JBT Corporation, and Hyster-Yale Group, I have consistently driven cost optimization initiatives, saving significant capital through precise analysis and strategic negotiations. I am skilled in advanced costing tools like aPriori and TCPLM, alongside developing robust Excel and Power BI models for detailed cost analysis and reporting.

I thrive in cross-functional team environments, excel in supplier negotiations across global markets (including the U.S., Mexico, Europe, and India), and am passionate about leveraging data and technology to enhance efficiency and decision-making in the manufacturing supply chain.

My commitment to continuous learning is reflected in my certifications and courses in areas like Lean Six Sigma, Risk Management (FMEA), and emerging technologies like AI/ChatGPT and Micro/Nano Fabrication Techniques.

Material Rates & Labor Benchmarks

  • Common Materials: Steel (Mild, Stainless, Alloy), Aluminum (6061, 7075), Copper, Brass
  • Special Alloys: Titanium, Inconel, Hastelloy (overview of cost drivers)
  • Plastic Resins: ABS, Polycarbonate, Nylon (overview for injection molding)
  • Labor Rate Benchmarks: Regional variations (e.g., India, US, Mexico, Europe) for skilled and semi-skilled labor in manufacturing.

Certification & Standards Overview

  • ISO 9001:2015: Quality Management Systems (QMS) - foundational for all manufacturing.
  • ISO/TS 16949: Automotive Quality Management - specific to automotive supply chain.
  • AS9100: Aerospace Quality Management Systems - critical for aerospace components.
  • ISO 13485: Medical Devices Quality Management - for medical device manufacturing.
  • FMEA (Failure Mode and Effects Analysis): Risk management tool for process and design.
  • PPAP (Production Part Approval Process): Standardized method for approving new or revised parts.

Logistics & Shipping Considerations

  • Packaging: Material costs, labor for packing, specialized packaging for sensitive components.
  • Tariffs & Duties: Impact of international trade agreements and regional specific duties on landed cost.
  • Freight: Air vs. Sea freight costs, lead times, fuel surcharges, and optimal shipping routes.
  • Incoterms: Understanding EXW, FOB, CIF, DDP, and their impact on cost responsibility.
  • Customs Clearance: Documentation, brokerage fees, and potential delays.
